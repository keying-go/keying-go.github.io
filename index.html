CV | Facial Expression Detection

The "Emphathizer" system detects human faces and recognizes emotions in real-time. For emotion recogniton, we implemented 4 models in total to classify the input facial expression images into one of the 7 categories (angry, disgust, fear, happy, neutral, sad, and surprise). We first implemented a self-designed Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) that achieved 63.19% accuracy. Then, to further improve the accuracy of expression detection, we leveraged transfer learning by having 3 backbone structures (VGG-16, MobileNet, and ResNet50) along with our own classification heads. The optimal structure achieved a test accuracy of 69.47%. Finally, we integrated the best model with our interface that reads the camera inputs and detects the face. Our system should be used with individuals' explicit consent. It should not be misused for malicious activities.

NLP | Ukrainian War Sentiment Analysis + Topic Discovery

To better understand English-speaking Twitter users' reactions to this conflict, we use bidirectional LSTM model to analyze the sentiments and use Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) to discover topics of the discussions. We also visualize our results to explore how the emotions change overtime. Combining the topic discovery results and sentiment analysis, we can explore possible effect of topics on emotions.

Fatal Police Shootings

On average, police in the United States shoot and kill more than 1,000 people every year. In this project, we analyze and predict 2022 fatal shootings using time-series model for the whole population and for different racial groups. Although half of the people shot and killed by police are White, Black Americans are shot at a disproportionate rate.
